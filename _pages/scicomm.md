Society benefits when decisions are supported by the best available science. Therefore, I think it is important for scientists, especially those supported by public funds, to help communicate scientific results to the public and to decision makers. This is especially important for topics of critical societal importance such as human-caused climate change but I think it is important for all aspects of science, including basic research. Many of the most societal useful scientific results came as the result of basic research aimed at a completely different topic, and it is important to both the public and to the practice (and funding) of science that the public understand the benefit of both applied and basic research.
